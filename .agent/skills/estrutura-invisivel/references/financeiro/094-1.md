     However, it also holds many stories and secrets of

She achieved sainthood after three visitations with the Virgin Mary, who instructed her to strike the Miraculous Medal so that those who wear it could receive God's graces. And though she died in 1876, today her body is as fresh as the day she was buried, her eyes still radiate their blue calmness, and her arms and legs are as supple as if she were asleep. Her incorrupt body is encased in glass in a chapel down a narrow alley next to one of Paris' illustrious department stores. It's a profoundly spiritual place, one of the city's best-kept secrets, and one you're unlikely to find in the guidebooks.       Here's another-- the world's first manned balloon flight took place between two Parisian villages: Passy (now a suburb in the city's 16th arrondissement) and Buttes-Aux Cailles (in the 13th arrondissement). And Benjamin Franklin, who was a highly celebrated Paris resident for nine years, was among those that witnessed this historic
