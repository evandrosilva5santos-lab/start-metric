     In 1999, Belize enacted some very attractive "retiree" legislation. I  use 
the term "retiree" loosely...as you have to be only 45 years of age to take advantage of the program's benefits. And there's almost no red tape or fine print to qualify.       In fact, to qualify, you need show only that you have a monthly income of as little as $1,000 (from a pension or other form of retirement income, or $2,000 per month from investment income).       In other countries offering these kinds of retiree programs, you must make a huge financial commitment before you can take advantage of the program's benefits. Preconditions can range from making large investments or buying expensive homes to putting hundreds of thousands of dollars into long-term CDs or other government financial instruments .
