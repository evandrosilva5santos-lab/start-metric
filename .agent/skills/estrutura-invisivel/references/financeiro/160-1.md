The Real First Great Depression   
The first real depression happened between 1837 and 18 43.  
Before the Civil War, most investment was financed throu gh debt. 
And just like what's happening today, an increasing su pply of debt 
became a source of economic instability.  America was an emerging economy, and states floated bon ds to 
finance major development projects. The glut of bonds flooding the 
market absorbed all domestic saving, and then some. The  result 
was falling bond prices.  
In the last half of 1841 and the first months of 1842  eight states and 
the Territory of Florida defaulted on their debts. I n January of 1842, 
the bank notes of most western states traded at discount s of 25 
percent or more in New York, and the notes of banks in  several 
states could not be sold at any price.  The Depression of 1837 was the worst depression in Amer ican 
history to that time, causing financial ruin and misery t o millions.  
Hundreds of banks and businesses failed and thousands of families 
lost their farms and homes.
