Now, what are  stock options?   
An option gives you the right -- but not the obligation -- to buy or sell a stock, index or future at a specific price for a specified amount of tim e.  
With options, you have 'the right to buy or sell a specific stock at an 
established price (known as the strike price) up to a specified date. Opt ions 
are bought and sold to take advantage of the price movement in the underlying stock.'  Say, for example, stock in XYZ Corp. is trading for $100 per share. 100 shares would cost you $10,000. The option  for that same stock, on the 
other hand, might be listed at 8 points. That means you can purchase the option -- and control 100 shares of the stock -- for as little as $800, not 
including commissions. As Steve points out, you are not buying the stock. You are 'leasing the profit potential of the stock for a given amount of time.'  An option to buy is a call option. 
  
You can profit even when the stock goes down!   
Another benefit of options is that they're flexible. You can profit in options 
even if the price goes down by purchasing the right to sell  a portion of an 
underlying stock, at a specified price within a specified time.That's known 
as a put option.   
If you believe a particular stock will drop in price, you can buy a put option and if you're right, profit from the drop in price of the underlying stock.  Page 5 of 17
