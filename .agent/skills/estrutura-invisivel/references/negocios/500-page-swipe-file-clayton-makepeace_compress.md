23¢ Life-Saver
Over the years I have watched a particular “A” level copywriter make 
a career out of headlines listing a very low price for a solution to a 
major problem. I borrowed this idea for a promotion of a client’s 
Oral Chelation product. 
It occurred to me that a heart bypass could cost over a hundred 
thousand dollars, yet there is evidence that a 23-cent capsule 
containing EDTA (the amino acid used in chelation therapy) opens 
arteries and prevents heart attacks.
This package is based on that concept. It is also a great example of 
how story telling early in the text can grab readers attention. Also 
notice page four, where we take the problem of clogged arteries 
out of the realm of the imagination and put it clearly in front of the 
prospect. I am absolutely convinced that if more promotions for 
health products demonstrated how a problem develops inside our 
bodies… helped people actually visualize the disease process… 
and “see” how a particular substance halts or reverses that process… 
health marketers would have greater success. 
We also use another proof element that has worked quite well for 
us: The concept that you can see it working. We found a way for 
customers to prove that artery plaque was actually being ﬂushed 
out of their bodies. So, armed with the mental image of how 
plaque forms and causes heart attacks and strokes … and then the 
oﬀering of a simple test proving the supplement worked, we had 
tremendous credibility.
Notice also that we don’t miss the opportunity to identify our 
common enemy, the drug companies, who would, if they had a 
patentable product that could do the same thing that Chelation 
could do, would charge you an arm and a leg for it. 
This package has also mailed in the millions. It was ﬁrst tested 
in spring of 2005 and it’s still being mailed aggressively across 
America.

GIVE ME 90 DA YS  
AND I’LL HELP YOU…
In the early 1990’s, a group publisher at Phillips Publishing asked if 
I would consider creating a launch promotion for Health & Healing 
— a new “alternative” health newsletter he planned to sell for $39.95 
per year.
I had little hope that the promotion would do well. There were no 
successful newsletters on alternative therapies at the time.
And while this product would sell on a “cash-with-order” basis, all 
the newsletters being published by major universities sold for just 
$29.95 and billed the subscriber after he had signed up.
But then again, I’ve always loved a challenge…
…So, I jetted oﬀ to California to meet with Dr. Whittaker. After a  
two or three hour meeting, I checked into a hotel and wrote the 
promotion you see here.
Within a week after this promotion mailed for the ﬁrst time, the 
publisher called again — this time, to say that he had his order  
processing department working three shifts just to count the  
money that was pouring in!
This promotion and two others I created for Health And Healing 
went on to mail 100 million pieces and generated nearly 2 million 
subscribers in just 3 years!
And what’s more, they enabled Phillips Publishing to found a  
division that formulated and marketed nutritional supplements… 
and generated hundreds of millions of dollars a year in additional 
revenues.

7 Horsemen
Here is an example of a package that mailed in the millions, using a 
headline that I adapted from the scriptures. 
The New Testament’s Book of Revelation talks about the Four 
Horsemen of the Apocalypse. My client Dr. Martin Weiss, had 
identiﬁed seven reasons why he was convinced that the economy 
was going to fall apart and the stock market was going to crash later 
in the year. 
My headline: “Seven Horsemen of the Coming Stock Market 
Apocalypse” . 
Most of us have experienced some religious teaching at some 
time in our lives. And key words and phrases from Bible stories, for 
example, tend to have greater visceral power for us. So we used one 
of those phrases as an attention-getting device on this package.
Also notice that on page four, we paint the picture that the collapse 
is inevitable: Not even the most powerful men in the political or 
ﬁnancial worlds could stop it. It reminded me of a promotion I 
wrote years ago saying, “All the King’s Horses and all the Kings Men 
Couldn’t Put the Economy Back Together Again. ” 
Notice the use of speciﬁcs throughout the package -- including a 
forecast that NASDAQ would hit 800 -- and other speciﬁcs, numbers, 
facts and ﬁgures to support the somewhat outrageous claim that 
the economy was going to crash. 
Also notice how we used Dr. Weiss’ press notices and other things 
to oﬀset skepticism that would be raised as prospects read these 
starting forecasts.

Shameless Two-Faced S.O.B.s!
If I had to choose a package that taught me valuable lessons in  
the Direct Mail Industry, I’d choose this one.
I had a ball writing it! The ﬁrst draft actually had photos of  
Bill Gates as well as the CEO’s of CISCO, GENZYME, SYMANTEC,  
SIEBEL SYSTEMS, RED HAT and YAHOO on the cover.
The company’s lawyers and compliance oﬃcer nearly had a stroke 
when they saw that draft. They said there was no way we could mail 
that promotion — and pronounced it dead on arrival.
But I refused to let the package die. Instead, I called the legal  
beagles and began haggling — and we found a middle ground  
that let the package be used.
I even got to include the photos of the errant CEOs — albeit on 
page 5.
Lesson learned: “Blindly taking dictation from lawyers WILL damage 
your response but if you call them and negotiate you WILL ﬁnd a happy 
medium. ”
Enjoy!

SUPERNET
This Supernet package may be the longest running control I’ve ever 
written for a ﬁnancial newsletter. It mailed successfully for three 
years, during some really tough markets. By positioning the internet 
as being obsolete and announcing the new “Supernet, ” — which is 
based on ﬁber optic cable and is many times faster than the internet 
— this package captured prospects’ attention in a powerful way.
It also seized on my prospect’s frustration with how slow the 
internet can be and oﬀered interesting news about the terabyte 
speeds possible over ﬁber optic cable. 
The promotion was very simple inside, just two-color for a more 
newsy feel. However; we used a four-color simulated wrap on the 
outside to sell prospects on reading the promotion.
We were also fortunate to have an advisor with an excellent track 
record. Even at a time when the NASDAQ was exploding higher; 
Tony was beating the the averages by a considerable amount.
On page twelve, we demonstrate that this is not simply a twelve-
issues-a-year deal. Subscribers would receive much, much more 
from us — 62 advisories per year.
On page fourteen, we take an early opportunity to show them 
everything that they are going to get. Quite often, the photograph 
showing all of the premiums and the other things that come along 
with a subscription doesn’t appear until just inside the back cover 
— page 22 on a 24 page self mailer. 
On page eighteen, instead of showing all of the products buyers 
get, we included testimonials from subscribers. This is an extremely 
important page in a direct mail promotion, as it faces the order 
form.

eventual loss of cognitive function.

relief for.

Arthur Johnson’s package. However; I had used several more pages 
than Arthur had used in his and therefore my mail cost was higher. 
We have since tested a shorter version of this piece, and the jury’s 
still out.

section of your market niche verses going to a very narrow section 
of it.

D deﬁcient. The headline was “The Silent Epidemic Killing Darker

This version of the package went after a broader group- everyone 
who’s worried about heart health.

niche was NOT more emotionally exercised than the larger niche.

responded better to the heart appeal than they did to the skin color 
appeal!

and worth testing.

handle testimonials to keep the constant drumbeat of customer 
experiences feeling fresh and exciting. We also used a process 
diagram to help prospects visualize what was going on inside them 
and how the product could help improve their health.

Choosing the main theme for an advertorial selling an investment 
newsletter product is always a dicey proposition.
Your challenge is to select a theme that will resonate with prospects 
… and that is tied to an economic or investment trend that is likely 
to continue for (hopefully) a year or more.
In late 2004, when this package was written for Weiss Research, it 
was clear to Larry Edelson — editor of Real Wealth Report — that 
rapidly rising federal deﬁcits would mean rising inﬂation and rising 
energy prices for years to come.
The only problem was, we were ahead of our time. Although Larry 
was 100% correct in his forecast… and despite the fact that the 
investments he recommended have since soared in value… our 
market was not yet on-board — and as a result, this package did not 
mail as well as we had hoped.
Nevertheless, it is an excellent example of how strong sales copy 
connected to a major trend can disappoint when you get ahead of 
your prospects.
