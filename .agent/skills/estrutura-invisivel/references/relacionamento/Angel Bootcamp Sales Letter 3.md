Just What Is “Angel Money” Anyway?  
 
If you haven’t spent a lot of time in the finance world (and you should count yourself 
lucky if you haven’t . . . it’s vicious) the term “Angel Money” might sound a little 
confusing.  
 
So let’s break it down.  
 
At its most basic, Angel Money is investment capital provided by a single affluent 
individual or  a group of individuals in exchange for a percentage of ownership equity in 
your company. (Venture Capitalists manage the pooled money of others in a 
professionally managed fund . . . and usually don’t get involved with a company until it’s 
past the startu p “Angel” phase.)  
 
Basically a wealthy  “Angel”  is giving you the funds you need to power your startup and 
growth in exchange for a percentage of the eventual profits the company creates . . . 
and a piece of the pie when and if the company sells.   
 
And dep ending on how much any one Angel puts in, they might have a lot or a little to 
say about the way you run your business. (One of the core things I teach people who

getting ac cess to Angel cash . . . after all, what’s the point of getting your company 
funded if you lose control of the whole thing?)  
 
Done right, Angel funding is an incredibly powerful business -building tool that can get 
your “big idea” off the ground, give you t he resources to dash ahead of competitors, cut 
years  off the time it takes you to grow, and get you on the path to profitability fast.
