How it All Began  
In 1984, just six months after GMI ﬁrst opened its doors for business, our 
president and founder Earl Overstreet was asked to provide a large data storage 
subsystem  for a team of scientists at the University of Washington Applied 
Physics Laboratory . The team needed  a way to store 11GB of data as it  was 
collected in the  harsh conditions of the  Arctic. The problem? The technology to 
hold that kind of information, to do it reliably and to do it in real time simply  hadn’t 
been developed for their computer system yet. So what did GMI do? We created 
it. 
By chaining together 11 write -once optical disk drives (each drive used a 12 inch 
1GB cartridge to store data), designing cables, picking an interface card and 
teaming with a local software company, we were able to put together a system 
that would stand up even to the UW ’s tough demands. The project went off 
smoothly, the system performed as it was designed and GMI’s reputation 
for developing creative solutions to complex storage issues was born.
